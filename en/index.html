The Breach Arrives as a Login

The most common digital exposure in a personal dispute doesn't involve a hacker, a virus, or a sophisticated attack. It involves access that was granted inside a relationship — and never revoked when the relationship changed.

WhatsApp Web

The session nobody logged out

He moved out in March. The desktop computer stayed. His WhatsApp Web session was still active on the shared home PC — every message he sent and received visible on the screen, including his conversations with his attorney about which accounts he planned to move before proceedings formalised. She took screenshots for four weeks before he realised the session was still open.

PlayStation Family Account → OneDrive

The gaming account that connected everything

They had set up a PlayStation family account so the children could play online. That account was linked to his Microsoft account, which was part of a Microsoft family group. His OneDrive — where he had been moving financial documents and records of an account she didn't know existed — was accessible through the same family group. She found it while looking for a school project the children had saved. He had believed it was private.
