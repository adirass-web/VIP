Competitive Intelligence Rarely Arrives From Outside

In the documented record of trade secret theft and client data loss, the threat almost never comes from an external attacker. It walks out the door with someone who had the login — a contractor, a departing employee, a partner who left before anyone thought to check what they still had access to.

Google Drive (Personal Account)

The contractor whose Drive held everything

A marketing contractor had worked with the company for two years, using her personal Google account for all shared documents. Every brief, every campaign plan, every creative file lived in a Drive folder she owned. When the contract ended, the company had no copies on their own systems — it was all in her account. She wasn't malicious. But she had everything: three years of strategy, creative, and client data, stored in a folder that was never transferred.

Shared Office Device

The iPad that was still logged in

The office had a shared iPad used for client demos. It was still signed into the founder's personal Apple ID. When a former employee borrowed it for a meeting that ran long, they spent an hour in a waiting room with full access to iCloud, a copy of Mail that had never been logged out, and the App Store history showing every tool and subscription the company was using. The iPad had been handed over as a practical convenience. Nobody had considered what it was still connected to.
