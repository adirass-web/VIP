Why it has to be in person

Most security services are remote. A technician connects to your device from another city, applies configurations they can't fully audit in your environment, and leaves a service portal open behind them. The moment anyone has remote access to your device — even to protect it — your threat model is compromised. A backdoor for a security provider is a backdoor for anyone.

The method here is different. Hardening is applied in your presence, on your timeline, with hardware you keep. Nothing enters your network that you haven't seen. Nothing leaves your hands. No remote tools, no cloud logs, no third parties — at any point, for any reason.

How it works

  • Every engagement starts from your specific threat — not a generic checklist.
  • Nothing leaves your presence. No remote tools, no cloud access, no third parties.
  • One point of contact from first assessment to Certificate of Hardening — directly.
