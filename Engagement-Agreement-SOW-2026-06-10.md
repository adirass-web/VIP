### 2. Definitions

- **Primary device** — your phone (iOS or Android): the device that holds your keys, wallet, identity, archive, location, and the second factor that authorises your other accounts. It is the core unit of every engagement.
- **Additional device** — any further device you use (laptop, tablet, desktop).
- **Account** — any online service, identity, or credential you control (email, cloud, financial, social, messaging, smart-home).
- **Control plane** — the router, network, and the accounts that command your home environment, through which connected devices are governed.
- **Household / Inner-Circle member** — a person, additional to you, brought within the engagement's scope by name.
- **The Standard** — the fixed Hardening Standard set out in Appendix A, applied to the then-current published baselines.
- **The Certificate** — the signed Certificate of Hardening (Appendix C), the deliverable of every engagement.
- **Records** — for the avoidance of doubt: we retain the signed Certificate and a minimal engagement record only. We never retain your data, your credentials, or images of your devices (see §10).

### 3. The Hardening Standard

Every engagement applies the same disciplined Standard (Appendix A). **The Standard does not change between tiers — only its reach does** (see §4). The Standard is mapped to recognised public baselines, including the NSA, NIST (incl. SP 800-213 for connected-home devices), the CIS Benchmarks and CIS Controls v8, the U.S. DISA Apple iOS/iPadOS STIG, the UK NCSC and NZ NCSC device-security guidance, EFF Surveillance Self-Defense (threat modelling and Android device guidance), the Amnesty International Security Lab high-risk-user guidance, and the Coalition Against Stalkerware. Specific device-level settings are applied to the **then-current** published baseline for each platform and OS version; the detailed working checklist is maintained internally and is deliberately not reproduced here, so that it cannot serve as a roadmap for an adversary and so the Standard stays current as platforms change.

### 4. Scope of this engagement

The selected tier governs how far across your world the Standard is applied:

- **Personal Shield** — one person (you) and your primary device, with all your accounts, fully hardened to the Standard.
- **Home Shield** — Personal, plus your **home control plane** (router, network segmentation, the accounts that command your connected home) and **up to two (2) additional personal devices**.
- **Inner Circle Shield** — the Personal scope applied to **up to three (3) named people**, each with their primary device and accounts, coordinated in one engagement.
- **Sovereign Shield** — bespoke: depth and breadth combined, the high-risk-individual modes by default (hardware keys, Apple Lockdown Mode / Google Advanced Protection), a physical counter-surveillance (TSCM) sweep, and Annual Watch + Priority Retainer included.

The reach of each measure-group by tier is set out in the **Tier Scope Matrix (Appendix B)**.

### 5. Out of scope (the boundary)

To keep this engagement honest, finite, and defensible, the following are expressly **out of scope** unless added in writing:

- **Connected / smart-home devices are *contained*, not individually hardened.** Most such devices cannot be meaningfully hardened; they are isolated by network segmentation and brought under control through their governing accounts (see Appendix A §6). We do not undertake per-device hardening of an open-ended number of smart devices.
- **Caps and overflow.** Work beyond the selected tier's caps (more people, more devices, the vehicle) is a priced add-on, agreed **before** any additional work (see §13).
- **No retrospective forensics, no investigations, no attribution, no expert testimony or court appearance.** We provide forward hardening and light triage ("we find how you are exposed"); we do not perform evidentiary forensic examinations or act as expert witnesses.
- **Defensive only.** We never access, monitor, alter, or act against any other person's devices, accounts, or communications. We will decline any such request.
- **Not legal, financial, tax, or insurance advice.**

### 6. Method

The engagement follows the Practice's disciplined sequence, in your presence, in one continuous engagement:

1. **Threat model** — we map your assets, your adversaries, and how each can reach you (Appendix A §1).
2. **Exposure triage** — we check for what may already be wrong: rogue sessions, unfamiliar logins, silent forwarding, stalkerware, leaked credentials (§2).
3. **Authentication hardening** — passkeys and hardware keys become the locks (§3).
4. **Device & OS hardening** — to the current baseline (§4).
5. **App, cloud & privacy configuration** — reviewed and closed, one by one (§5).
6. **Network & connected environment** — *(Home and above)* the control plane brought back under your sole command (§6).
7. **Coaching** — the habits, the key, the secure channel (§9).
8. **Certificate of Hardening** — the signed record for your file (§7, Appendix C).

### 7. Deliverable — the Certificate of Hardening

The engagement concludes with a signed, dated **Certificate of Hardening** (Appendix C): a record of every measure applied and every standard referenced, suitable for your own records and your attorney's file. It records *what was done*, never the content of your data or accounts.

### 8. Client responsibilities & prerequisites

To complete the engagement in the booked time, you agree to provide: your physical presence and that of any named member; access to the in-scope devices and their passcodes; the administrator credentials for in-scope accounts and the home router; a current backup of each device (we can advise beforehand); and an available decision-maker for the day.

### 9. The Promise (binding terms)

These are commitments, not slogans. For this engagement:

- **In person only** — all work is performed physically, in your presence.
- **No remote access** — we never connect to or control your devices remotely.
- **No cloud** — we never upload or process your data in any cloud service.
- **No logs** — we keep no logs of your data, credentials, or device contents.
- **No app** — nothing of ours is installed on, or left resident on, your devices.

### 10. Confidentiality & data handling

Each party will keep the other's confidential information in confidence; this obligation is mutual and survives the engagement. We retain only the signed Certificate and a minimal engagement record (your name, tier, date). **We retain no client data, no credentials, and no device images.** Where you are represented, we will, at your direction, coordinate with your attorney so that relevant communications may be handled under legal privilege. Contact is by WhatsApp or Signal only; no forms, no third-party intermediaries.

### 11. Safety & sequencing protocol

If, during triage, we find active surveillance, stalkerware, or an existing compromise, **immediate removal can alert a hostile party and escalate the risk to you** — physically or legally. In that event we will not act reflexively. We will assess whether and when to remediate or to contain; sequence any action to protect your safety first; and, where you may wish to consult your attorney, advise you on preserving *your own* records — without our conducting forensics or providing testimony (§5). We proceed only on your informed decision. *(This capability is described, in principle, on our website; the operational detail lives here.)*

## Appendix A — The Hardening Standard (named measures)

*The fixed spine, applied to the then-current published baseline for each platform. This named-measures version is the basis of the public "What's included" page; detailed settings are internal.*

1. **Threat model & risk assessment** — your assets, adversaries, likelihood, consequence, and the effort each adversary can bring; the plan that follows from it. *(EFF Surveillance Self-Defense — "Your Security Plan".)*
2. **Exposure triage / digital counter-surveillance** — review for rogue sessions and unfamiliar logins; silent mail-forwarding and auto-reply rules; **stalkerware and hidden tracking**; and leaked-credential / breach exposure. *(Coalition Against Stalkerware; Amnesty International Security Lab; Have I Been Pwned.)* — **in every tier.**
3. **Identity & authentication** — strong unique passphrases in a password manager; multi-factor authentication moved off SMS to **hardware security keys and passkeys**; account-recovery hardening; a separate, private layer for account control. *(NCSC NZ; FIDO2 / passkeys; NIST SP 800-63B.)*
4. **Device & operating-system hardening** — supported OS and current updates; device encryption; passcode/biometric policy, auto-lock and erase-on-failure; Find My / remote wipe; encrypted backups; jailbreak/root check; lock-screen exposure. *(CIS Benchmarks; DISA Apple iOS/iPadOS STIG / NIAP MDFPP; EFF Surveillance Self-Defense (Android); NCSC platform guides.)*
5. **App, cloud & privacy configuration** — per-app permissions; location and family sharing; shared albums and backups; cloud-account security; browser hardening; social-media privacy; reduction of your data-broker footprint. *(NCSC; CIS; industry practice.)*
6. **Home network & connected environment** *(Home and above)* — router firmware, admin credentials and WPA3; **network segmentation into Trusted / Guest / IoT with default-deny**; control of the smart-home hub accounts; **containment** of connected devices. *(NIST SP 800-213; CIS Controls v8.)*
7. **Vehicle** *(add-on)* — connected-car account, paired devices, and location/telemetry brought under your control.
8. **Physical & travel** — device physical-security habits; public-Wi-Fi and USB-charging discipline; **Apple Lockdown Mode / Google Advanced Protection** where the threat model warrants; **physical counter-surveillance (TSCM) sweep** *(Sovereign / by arrangement)*. *(NCSC NZ; Apple; Google; Amnesty International Security Lab; TSCM practice.)*
9. **Coaching & habits** — recognising phishing and social engineering; using the secure channel; the routines that keep the hardening durable. *(NCSC NZ.)*
10. **Certificate of Hardening** — the signed, dated record (Appendix C).
