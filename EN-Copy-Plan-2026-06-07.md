## 0. Resolved decisions (locked 2026-06-07)

- **Temporal persona (new axis):** every page addresses both stages — **precaution** (planning ahead) and **post-incident** (already exposed) — in sequence on one page (IA choice 3a). Both funnel to the ₪3,500 Threat Assessment.
- **Service boundary (1b):** forward hardening + **light triage** — we may say "we find *how* you're exposed," but NOT claim retrospective forensic reconstruction of a past breach. Guardrail against overpromise.
- **Hero priority (2a):** dual-track, kept sharp by leading with the stage-agnostic revelation.
- **Provocation (4a):** revelation via true stories — vignettes do the waking; frame stays calm. No urgency language.
- **Stakes section reframed:** the point is **"nobody had to hack anything"** — a spouse, business counterpart, or private investigator exploits mundane, often legal-ish vectors (open sessions, family/shared plans, location sharing, cloud backups, known PINs, reused passwords). State-grade hacking is unnecessary. This replaces the weaker "access not a hack" framing.

### Name policy (locked) — Dr. Tabansky
Positional, not absolute. The name is **out of all heroes** (an unrecognised name does no persuasion work on a cold visitor). It first appears where credibility is being built — Why Us, the method, the Certificate, contact — and in meta descriptions for SEO. Heroes use "we." Rationale: brand promises the category; the man supplies the rarity — but only once the reader is past the hook.

### Homepage hero — LOCKED
- Kicker (small-caps gold): **Your phone knows everything.**
- Headline: **When your private life is contested, the first move is closing the doors you didn't know were open.**
- Sub: **In a separation, a business split, or a contested estate, the person across from you rarely needs to break in. We find how you're exposed and shut it — discreetly, in person, in a single day.**

## A. Template logic — why these two pages

The homepage is the lobby (multi-situation, authority-first). The situation page is the **product/persona** — where an intent-less or referred visitor is convinced. Everything else clones one of these: the 4 other situation pages clone separation-divorce; /pricing and the attorney section are downstream. Get these two right and rollout is mechanical.

### Situation hero — headline options (separation-divorce)
- **A (recommended):** "Going through a separation? Secure every device and account — in one day, before it's used against you." *Plain, situation-named, calm resolution, mild stakes.*
- **B:** "In a divorce, the breach is rarely a hacker. It's the login nobody closed." *Leads with the insight; pairs into the vignettes.*
- **C:** "Protect what's private before the proceedings begin." *Quietest; most white-glove, least specific.*

### Depth policy applied (the tension, resolved)
- Section 2 gets **brief logic, not an essay**: 3 concrete exposure types, one sentence each — enough to make it real, short enough to stay calm.
- Section 3 keeps vignettes near-verbatim (they're earned, specific, true).
- **External stories: selective** — at most one short, sourced "this is a known pattern" line in §2 if it materially raises belief; never at the cost of the page's calm. Anything researched gets a citation in the plan before it ships.

## D. Voice rules in force (from positioning §5, §7)

Always "your Personal Cyber Shield / the Shield." "We," Dr. Tabansky named at trust moments. No fear-pumping, no countdown urgency, no "they are already snooping." Defensive framing only. Single prices. No invented stats. Simple words; active voice; no exclamation points; benefits tied to the client's situation, not features.
