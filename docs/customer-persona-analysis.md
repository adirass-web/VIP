# Customer Persona Analysis
## VIP In-Person Digital Security — Crisis Hardening Service

*Prepared April 2026. Service context: in-person-only device and account hardening (₪9,000+ entry), no cloud, no remote access, no logs. Four crisis verticals: Separation/Divorce, Business Dispute, Inheritance Clash, Commercial Spying.*

---

## Strategic Frame: Who Is Actually Buying This?

The service is not bought by people who are merely interested in security. It is bought by people who are already inside a crisis, who have already received legal advice, and who have just realized — or been told — that their devices are a liability. The purchase trigger is almost always external: an attorney says "protect your phone before you file," or a partner calls unexpectedly with accusations, or a founder finds out a competitor has information they shouldn't. The buyer is not choosing between security vendors. They are choosing between acting and not acting, today.

This frames every persona: they are not security-conscious in the abstract. They are frightened of a specific outcome that could happen in the next 72 hours.

## Persona 1 — "The Divorcing Executive"
*Primary vertical: Separation & Divorce*

### Who They Are

**Name archetype:** Roni / Rachel / Rina  
**Age:** 42–58  
**Gender:** Skews female in contested property divorces; male in business-asset divorces  
**Location:** Tel Aviv, Herzliya Pituah, Raanana, or Jerusalem affluent districts. Some Russian-speaking diaspora in central Israel.  
**Occupation:** C-suite or senior professional (VP, partner at law firm or medical practice, real estate investor, business owner). Not necessarily tech-literate but tech-reliant.  
**Net worth:** ₪5M–₪30M. Divorce is over real money — property, business interests, retirement accounts, custody arrangements tied to financial outcomes.  
**Languages:** Hebrew primary; Russian-speaking subgroup (émigré professionals, 45–65) operates partly in Russian; English-speaking international subgroup (dual citizens, expats) uses EN pages.

### Psychographics

They are private by nature — not secretive in a criminal sense, but deeply protective of their personal life. They have always kept business and personal separate. The idea of their spouse's attorney reading their WhatsApp messages makes them feel physically sick. They have probably already Googled "can my spouse see my iCloud?" at 2am.

They trust recommendations from other professionals (their attorney referred them, or a friend who went through a divorce mentioned the service). They do not respond to ads but they do respond to authority and specificity. The phrase "Pentagon-grade" lands because they have no idea what that means technically, but it sounds like the highest available standard.

They are already spending ₪50,000–₪300,000+ on legal fees. ₪9,000 for a one-day hardening session is trivial compared to the cost of evidence being weaponized in court. They do not negotiate on price; they negotiate on timing and trust.

### Unmet Needs

- **Emotional:** They need to feel that someone is handling the digital dimension with the same discretion their attorney handles the legal dimension. They want an expert who will not judge them, will not ask unnecessary questions, and will not leave any trace of having been there.
- **Functional:** They do not know which apps share data with the cloud, which backups can be subpoenaed, or how to disable shared photo albums without alerting their spouse. They need someone to walk them through this in person without them having to understand it themselves.
- **Informational:** They have received vague legal advice ("protect your digital assets") but no practical map of what that means. The gap between "my lawyer said be careful" and knowing what to actually do is exactly where this service lives.

### Behaviors

- Contacts via WhatsApp (not email — email feels too traceable, too formal)
- Books within 24–48 hours of first contact; urgency is already present
- Wants the session done before a specific legal milestone (filing date, deposition, mediation session)
- Asks "who else will know about this?" before anything else
- Pays in a single transfer; no installments, no invoices addressed to shared accounts
- May want a separate device for post-separation communications (the "Additional Device" tier)

### Willingness to Pay

**Entry:** ₪9,000 (Core Shield) without significant friction.  
**Ceiling:** ₪15,000–₪20,000 for comprehensive coverage (additional devices, home/office sweep) if the attorney has already told them the risk is high.  
**Price sensitivity:** Very low. The comparison set is not "other security services" — it is "what it costs if the other side gets this information." That number runs into millions.

### Key Message That Converts

*"Before you hand over a single document or sit across from your ex's attorney — let us lock down everything they could use against you. You'll be with us the entire time. Nothing leaves the room."*

## Persona 2 — "The Betrayed Business Partner"
*Primary vertical: Business Dispute*

### Who They Are

**Name archetype:** Dov / Dmitri / Daniel  
**Age:** 38–62  
**Gender:** Predominantly male; mixed in partnerships with female founders  
**Location:** Tel Aviv, Petah Tikva industrial/tech areas, or Russian-speaking business communities in Ashdod/Netanya  
**Occupation:** Founder, co-founder, managing director, or senior partner in an SME (₪5M–₪100M revenue). Not a corporate employee — this is their company, their money, their reputation.  
**Languages:** Hebrew for Israeli-born business owners; Russian for post-Soviet immigrant entrepreneurs who built businesses in Israel but still conduct some business in Russian.

### Psychographics

They built something real and they are watching a partner, investor, or competitor try to take it. They feel personally violated — this is not just business, it is existential. They have already spoken to an attorney and probably an accountant. They are in crisis mode but still trying to project control.

They are highly skeptical of anyone new in their circle right now. They trust this service because it arrived through a recommendation (an attorney, an accountant, or a mutual contact), not because they found it on Google. The "in-person only, no cloud, no logs" positioning directly addresses their biggest fear: that hiring a security service creates a new information leak.

They want the service provider to be invisible. They do not want anyone on their team to know this is happening. They may schedule the session at their home or a neutral location rather than their office.

### Unmet Needs

- **Immediate:** Lock down all devices and communications channels before the next legal or commercial event (a board meeting, a negotiation session, a legal filing).
- **Strategic:** Create a clean communications channel for their attorney and core team that cannot be compromised.
- **Psychological:** Restore a sense of control. Right now they feel exposed. The act of having their devices hardened is itself partially therapeutic — it is a concrete action they can take.

### Behaviors

- Contacts via Signal (more likely than WhatsApp; higher paranoia)
- Will ask technical questions — "what does FIDO2 mean?" — not because they need to understand it but because they need to assess whether the provider is credible
- May bring a trusted colleague or their attorney to the initial diagnostic call
- Wants the work done over a weekend or outside business hours
- Will refer the service to their attorney within 30 days of a successful engagement ("my guy handled the digital side")

### Willingness to Pay

**Entry:** ₪9,000 without much discussion.  
**Ceiling:** ₪25,000–₪40,000 for leadership team + office sweep + ongoing protocol consulting.  
**Price sensitivity:** Low for themselves; moderate when paying for staff devices. The CEO will authorize the full package; finance will try to trim the staff devices.

### Key Message That Converts

*"Your former partner already knows your patterns. Let us change everything they think they know about your communications — in one day, in person, with no trace of the engagement."*

## Persona 3 — "The Inheritance Mediator"
*Primary vertical: Inheritance Clash*

### Who They Are

**Name archetype:** Miriam / Mikhail / Maya  
**Age:** 50–72 (the person managing the estate or the primary beneficiary); the sibling conflict is often among 40–65-year-olds  
**Gender:** Mixed; women often take on estate executor roles in Israeli families  
**Location:** Spread across Israel; often involves family members in multiple cities or countries (common in Russian-speaking post-Soviet families with Israeli citizenship)  
**Occupation:** Professional (doctor, attorney, accountant) or retired business owner acting as executor; sometimes a family member with limited tech literacy who has been handed responsibility  
**Languages:** Russian is particularly prominent here — Russian-speaking families with significant assets in Israel often have multi-generational wealth disputes that span borders.

### Psychographics

This persona is slightly older and less comfortable with technology than Personas 1 and 2. They have been handed a legal responsibility (executor, power of attorney holder, primary beneficiary) that requires them to manage documents and communications at a level they are not used to. They are worried about making a mistake that costs the family money or relationships.

They are often caught between two forces: pressure from an attorney to be "more organized" about documents, and pressure from family members who may be hostile or may be trying to access accounts they should not. The digital threat is often internal — a sibling trying to access a parent's email account, a family member changing beneficiary designations.

The no-cloud, no-logs positioning is important here because they worry about lawyers and courts having access to everything. They want to handle the estate privately.

### Unmet Needs

- **Protective:** Prevent family members from accessing estate accounts, modifying documents, or manipulating elderly parents' devices.
- **Organizational:** Create a clean, secure system for managing estate documents that their attorney can trust.
- **Educational:** Understand what the digital exposure actually is — they often do not know what "the cloud" means in practice.

### Behaviors

- May be referred by the estate attorney directly ("call this number before you log into the account")
- First contact is often by phone rather than WhatsApp — they are less comfortable with messaging apps
- Sessions often involve a family member present, not just the executor
- May need guidance extended to elderly parents who own the devices in question

### Willingness to Pay

**Entry:** ₪9,000, but may need the value explained more carefully — they are not as instinctively calibrated to security spending as Personas 1 and 2.  
**Ceiling:** ₪15,000 for multiple family devices.  
**Price sensitivity:** Moderate. The comparison is to attorney fees, which they are already paying heavily. Once framed as "this protects the attorney's work," it converts.

### Key Message That Converts

*"Before anyone logs into your parents' accounts or starts 'helping' with the estate — lock down access. One day, in person. Everything stays in the family."*

## Persona 4 — "The Exposed Founder"
*Primary vertical: Commercial Spying*

### Who They Are

**Name archetype:** Ariel / Alexei / Avi  
**Age:** 32–52  
**Gender:** Skews male; female in mixed founding teams  
**Location:** Tel Aviv tech ecosystem (Rothschild corridor, Herzliya industrial zone), or Russian-speaking entrepreneur community  
**Occupation:** Startup founder, scaleup CEO, or SME owner in a competitive sector (tech, logistics, real estate development, import/export). Company has real IP — proprietary code, customer lists, supplier agreements, M&A deal flow.  
**Languages:** English-speaking founders (often with international investors or acquirers) use EN pages. Israeli-born tech founders use HE. Russian-speaking entrepreneurs use RU.

### Psychographics

This persona is the most tech-aware of the four but also the most overconfident — they have been meaning to "do something about security" for months and have not gotten around to it. The trigger for contacting the service is a specific incident: a competitor showed up at a client meeting with information they should not have had; a term sheet detail leaked before it was announced; a key employee left and joined a rival with suspicious timing.

They are fast movers. They make decisions quickly. The "Rapid Response — on-site in 1–3h" tier exists specifically for this persona at the moment of maximum urgency.

They respond strongly to outcome-language ("zero leaks in negotiations") and to specificity ("M&A threads, key-customer deals, and IP files"). They do not need hand-holding on why this matters — they need to believe you can actually do it.

They are also the most likely to refer the service aggressively within their network if the engagement impresses them. Their referral is worth 3–5 future clients.

### Unmet Needs

- **Immediate response:** When the suspected incident happens, they want someone on-site within hours, not days.
- **IP lockdown:** Protect the specific assets that make the company valuable — source code repositories, customer data, M&A communications.
- **Separation:** They are increasingly worried their personal phone (which has investor and customer communications) is mixed into a work environment that has been compromised.

### Behaviors

- Contacts via Signal, sometimes directly from an investor or board member's referral
- Will describe the incident in technical terms; wants a technically credible response, not reassurance
- Often authorizes the Rapid Response tier first (₪4,000) and then the full Core Shield within a week
- May want to extend hardening to a co-founder or CTO's devices in the same engagement
- Will ask for an assessment of what has likely already been accessed

### Willingness to Pay

**Entry:** ₪9,000 is trivially priced relative to a compromised M&A deal (deals at risk are typically ₪10M–₪200M+).  
**Ceiling:** ₪50,000+ for team-wide hardening, office/server room sweep, ongoing protocol consulting.  
**Price sensitivity:** Essentially none in a live incident. Somewhat more considered in a proactive engagement.

### Key Message That Converts

*"Rapid response, on-site within three hours. We find what's exposed, lock it down, and set up a clean channel before your next call with the acquirer."*

## Cross-Persona Synthesis

### What All Four Personas Share

**They are already in crisis.** No one books this service speculatively. The purchase event follows a trigger — a legal filing, a confrontation, a suspicious incident, an attorney's instruction. This means marketing must reach them during the window between trigger and action, which is short (24–72 hours in most cases).

**They value discretion above almost everything else.** "No cloud, no remote access, no logs" is not just a technical specification — it is an emotional promise. It says: *hiring us does not create a new vulnerability.* This is the core objection that must be preemptively answered for every persona.

**They are referred.** The primary acquisition channel is not SEO or paid social. It is attorneys, accountants, mediators, and former clients. The site exists to receive warm referrals and convert them — not to generate cold traffic. This has implications for the site's copy (it should feel like it is speaking to someone who already knows why they are there) and for where marketing energy should go (the referral network, not the SERP).

**They pay without negotiating.** The price is not questioned because it is small relative to what is at stake. Objections, when they arise, are about trust ("who else knows about this?") and timing ("can you come tomorrow?"), not cost.

**They contact via WhatsApp or Signal, not email.** The primary CTA placement (WhatsApp / Signal sticky) is correct. Email feels too traceable, too slow, too formal for someone in crisis mode.

### Pricing Architecture Assessment

The current tier structure (Core Shield ₪9,000 / Additional Device ₪3,000 / Rapid Response ₪4,000) is well-calibrated for Personas 1, 2, and 4. Persona 3 (Inheritance) may benefit from a "Family Bundle" framing — 2 devices at a stated combined rate — to match the multi-member household reality of estate situations.

The absence of a retainer/ongoing tier is notable. Personas 2 and 4 are the most likely to want a quarterly check-in or "clean channel maintenance" offering. This is a natural upsell that aligns with their ongoing legal/M&A processes.
