## What the Website Signals About Its Audience

Before naming personas, every element of the site encodes assumptions about who is reading it. Reading those signals carefully:

**The price points name the wealth bracket.** Entry is ₪15,000. The middle tier is ₪22,000. There is a "Sovereign Shield" at ₪95,000. The Threat Assessment at ₪3,500 is explicitly positioned as a credited entry point, not a standalone product. These are not consumer software prices — they are professional-services prices for people who are already spending heavily on attorneys, accountants, or forensic experts in an active dispute.

**The value comparisons name the dispute size.** The site says:
- "One leaked conversation in a ₪5M dispute costs more than this entire engagement" (divorce)
- "One leaked email in a ₪10M business dispute costs more than this entire engagement" (business)
- "A single IP leak costs your company millions" (commercial spying)
- "costs less than one day of your legal team" (commercial spying)
- "A leaked will turned a multi-million shekel dispute into a disaster" (inheritance)

These are not hypotheticals. They are calibration statements telling the reader: *if these numbers feel small compared to what you're dealing with, this page is for you.* The implied baseline is a dispute worth ₪5M–₪200M+.

**The threat language names the adversary.** In every vertical the threat is not "hackers" or "cybercriminals" — it is a specific person in the client's life:
- "They are already snooping" (spouse in divorce)
- "Greed, betrayal, and desperation make former partners ruthless" (former business partner)
- "Once rivals access your phone" (competing family member in inheritance)
- "When competitors breach your personal accounts" (business rival)

This is personal. Intimate. The person trying to get into your phone knows your patterns. The site writes directly to this emotional reality.

**The contact method signals paranoia.** WhatsApp and Signal exclusively — no phone number, no form, no email. The site even has WhatsApp prefill text in the URL so the visitor doesn't have to compose the first message. This is a service for people who are afraid of leaving a record of having sought help.

**The Certificate of Hardening signals attorney involvement.** Every tier includes "a signed Certificate of Hardening — professional confirmation for your legal file." The client already has a legal file. They already have an attorney.

**The three languages signal distinct communities.** Hebrew (Israeli nationals in all four verticals), Russian (post-Soviet diaspora with significant assets in Israel — the inheritance vertical specifically mentions "land registry," a common asset class for this community), English (expats, dual nationals, international founders, or anyone in the commercial spying / M&A vertical with international investors or acquirers).

**The scarcity note names capacity.** "Dr. Tabansky works with 3–6 clients per month." This is a deliberate exclusivity signal — not for everyone, not scalable, appointment-only.

**The FAQ "Can you hack my partner back?" names the emotional state.** This question is preemptively answered because some visitors arrive in a state of rage or desperation where it is a genuine impulse. They are not thinking about security — they are thinking about retaliation. The service positions itself as legal defense, not offense.

## Persona 1 — The Spouse About to File

*Primary page: /separation-divorce*

### What the site tells us

The divorce page opens: *"Before you share documents or emails — lock down your phone and accounts in one day."* The timing is before a legal action, not during or after. The hero anchor: *"One leaked conversation in a ₪5M dispute costs more than this entire engagement."* This is a person with meaningful assets at stake — property, business interests, financial accounts, possibly children.

The threat list is visceral and specific: "Use your photos and messages against you in court. Track your location and movements. Hijack backups and steal money from joint accounts." The intimacy of this threat (photos, location tracking, joint accounts) tells us the adversary is someone who knows the client's daily habits — a spouse or partner, not a stranger.

The deliverable "Profile and device separation for shared custody situations" signals children are in the picture. The "secure channel for you, family, and attorneys" — plural attorneys — suggests an ongoing legal process with multiple parties.

The Extended Shield covers "a second device (laptop, tablet, or child's device)." The possibility of hardening a child's device is a significant emotional signal. This is a parent.

### Inferred profile

A person in the process of ending a significant marriage or partnership — significant enough that assets are contested, attorneys are engaged on both sides, and the other party's behaviour has already crossed into surveillance or hostility. They have children (implied by custody device language). They have real assets (₪5M+ framing). They are not technically sophisticated — they don't know how to protect themselves and need someone to do it entirely for them, in person.

The multilingual page suggests this persona exists in Hebrew (Israeli-born), Russian (post-Soviet diaspora couple dissolving a marriage), and English (expat professional or dual national).

**Age signal:** The site uses no age-specific language, but the mention of a ₪5M dispute, legal proceedings, shared custody, and property (joint accounts, backups containing financial data) implies a mid-career to late-career individual — 38–58 is a reasonable inference.

**Urgency signal:** "Before you share documents" — the trigger is an imminent legal event, not a general anxiety. This person has a deadline.

## Persona 2 — The Business Owner in a Partnership Break

*Primary page: /business-dispute*

### What the site tells us

"Before negotiations or disagreements — harden devices and accounts in one day." Again, the timing is before a specific event. The threat framing: "Greed, betrayal, and desperation make former partners ruthless." Not just "competitors" — former partners. Someone who was on the inside.

The threat list confirms: "Weaponize your private communications in negotiations. Harvest trade secrets and strategy from your devices. Sabotage your financial standing and raid joint accounts." Joint accounts again — this former partner has or had shared financial access. This is a co-founder, a managing partner in a professional firm, or a co-owner of a closely held business.

The recommended tier for this vertical is the Extended Shield (₪22,000) — "protection for both business and personal devices." This person has mixed personal/professional life on their devices. Business email on their personal phone. Client data on a personal laptop. The lines are blurred, which is exactly what makes them vulnerable.

Features called out: "Hardware key + business / personal profile separation," "Secure channel for negotiations and core team," "Drive, SharePoint, and business email." These are the tools of a small-to-medium business owner or professional services partner — not a corporate employee whose IT department manages their devices.

The Sovereign Shield (₪95,000) includes an office sweep for "cameras, meeting rooms." This is someone with physical office space and staff.

The anchor price comparison: *"One leaked email in a ₪10M business dispute costs more than this entire engagement."* The dispute is at least ₪10M.

### Inferred profile

A founder, co-founder, managing partner, or majority/minority shareholder in a closely held business — partnership dispute is the central conflict. The business is worth ₪10M+. The adversary is someone who was an insider (knew the communications patterns, the file locations, the email habits). They have a small team and their own office. They are probably already in legal proceedings or about to enter them.

**Tech signal:** The business page mentions SharePoint specifically — this could be a more corporate-adjacent environment, or a professional services firm (law, accounting, consulting) using Microsoft infrastructure. Less consumer-grade than the divorce persona.

**Gender signal:** The site is gender-neutral throughout. The threat language ("former partners") is non-gendered.

## Persona 3 — The Executor or Primary Beneficiary in an Inheritance Fight

*Primary page: /inheritance-clash*

### What the site tells us

"Run inheritance disputes on facts — not leaks." The anchor: *"A leaked will turned a multi-million shekel dispute into a disaster. Don't be next."* The hero image (from the Unsplash slug) suggests a formal, somber context.

The threat language shifts from "they are snooping" to a specific threat about documents: "Seize control over documents, wills, and powers of attorney. Gain access to your attorney communications. Manipulate family members by twisting your private information." The adversary is inside the family — a sibling, a disputed co-beneficiary, or a party contesting the will.

The recommended tier is the Family Shield (from ₪29,000) — covering "2–4 family members / estate managers." This is the only vertical that names a multi-person deployment as the typical choice. The hardening extends to multiple people with different roles: "Hardware keys for executors and estate managers." The executor role is specific — this person has been legally appointed to manage an estate, which implies they are handling significant assets and are the target of pressure from others with competing claims.

Accounts secured: "email, cloud, banking, land registry." Land registry (Tabu in Hebrew/Israeli context) is a highly Israel-specific asset class — real estate held in the Israel Land Registry. This person's dispute almost certainly involves Israeli real estate.

### Inferred profile

An executor, estate administrator, or primary beneficiary managing a contested inheritance. The estate includes real property (land registry mention). Multiple family members are involved — some cooperative, some adversarial. The primary threat is document manipulation or unauthorized account access by a family rival. They may be managing elderly parents' devices (implied by "manipulation of family members"). They are probably not technically sophisticated — they were handed a legal responsibility and need expert help to protect the process.

The Russian-language page for this vertical is telling. Inheritance disputes in post-Soviet diaspora communities in Israel often involve complex multi-country asset structures, family members in Russia/Ukraine/FSU, and property acquired during the Soviet period. This is likely the deepest Russian-language vertical.

**Age signal:** The executor or primary beneficiary in a parental estate dispute is typically in their late 40s–60s. The multi-generational framing (parents' estate, siblings disputing) suggests a middle generation dealing with assets accumulated by an older generation.

## Persona 4 — The Executive or Founder Under Competitive Threat

*Primary page: /commercial-spying*

### What the site tells us

The hero headline is the most ROI-driven of all four: *"A single IP leak costs your company millions. This protection costs less than one day of your legal team."* This person thinks in terms of company value, legal team billing rates, and M&A deals.

The threat: "Weaponize your private communications in negotiations. Harvest trade secrets, IP, and R&D from your devices. Preempt your market strategy and product launches." The adversary is a competitor — external, not inside the family or the former partnership. But they have somehow gotten close enough to be credible: "When competitors breach your personal accounts, they gain access to your leadership's most sensitive communications."

Deliverables call out: "M&A threads, key-customer deals, and IP files." M&A explicitly. This is a company in a transaction — acquisition, fundraising, or a significant commercial negotiation.

The "What You Receive" section mentions: "Supplier and consultant access governance." This person has a vendor network. People outside the core team have access to some data.

The process step: "Pentagon-grade device configurations applied in your presence." This page is the only one that uses "Pentagon-grade" in the process description — it is the most technically-inflected page, pitched to someone who has heard of security standards.

The Rapid Response add-on is most prominently placed here (+₪1,500–₪6,000 "for suspected breach"). The commercial spying page is the one where the incident may have already occurred — the need for urgent, same-day response is highest.

### Inferred profile

A startup founder, scaleup CEO, or SME owner in a competitive sector — technology, logistics, real estate development, or any business with meaningful IP. Currently in a transaction or competitive negotiation (M&A mention is significant). Has a leadership team, suppliers, and consultants. The breach is suspected or confirmed — they may have already noticed anomalies (a competitor showing up with information they shouldn't have; a term sheet detail that leaked). This is the persona most likely to need rapid response.

English-language page is strong here — international investors, acquirers, and startup ecosystems operate in English even in Israel. This is likely the most English-dominant vertical.

**Universal:** Urgency is baked into every page. "Before you share documents." "Before negotiations." "Every hour you stay exposed is an hour you risk losing control." The trigger has already happened — the purchase decision is time-sensitive.

## Implications for Paid Advertising

### What the site signals about current acquisition

The site does not have a blog, content marketing, or editorial section. There is no lead magnet. There is no newsletter signup. The site's function is a conversion landing page, not a discovery vehicle. Someone arrives already knowing they need this kind of service — they are seeking the right person to provide it.

This architecture tells you paid ads should target people who are already in the crisis moment, not trying to create awareness of a category they don't yet know exists.

### Best-fit paid channels (inferred from public page signals)

**Google Search — the crisis-intercept channel.** The site's own copy gives you the search queries: "before you share documents," "before negotiations," "leaked will," "leaked email." People in these situations Google their fears. They search "can my spouse see my WhatsApp," "device security business dispute Israel," "protect phone before court." Google Search is the one channel that catches them at that exact moment.

**LinkedIn — the attorney/legal professional channel.** Every page assumes the client already has an attorney. The attorney is the trusted intermediary. A LinkedIn presence targeting family law attorneys, estate attorneys, forensic accountants, and mediators in Israel places Dr. Tabansky in front of the referring professionals — before the client ever searches.

**Google Remarketing — the deliberation window.** Visitors who were sent to the site by an attorney, spent time reading, and didn't contact immediately are in a deliberation window. Remarketing keeps the service visible without intrusion.

**Meta/Facebook — contraindicated.** A privacy-first service advertising on a platform notorious for data surveillance is a brand contradiction that sophisticated HNW clients will notice and distrust.
