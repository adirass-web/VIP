# Pricing Architecture
## VIP In-Person Digital Security — Personal Cyber Shield

*Prepared April 2026. Solo-operator practice, Dr. Tabansky. Associates available for expanded engagements. Individual-first focus: Separation/Divorce and Inheritance Clash as primary verticals. Business Dispute and Commercial Spying secondary.*

---

## Section 1 — Diagnosis: What Is Wrong with the Current Architecture

### The Core Problem: Scope Swallows Margin

The current entry tier shows "8–21 hours" on the site. That is a 2.6× range for the same published price. At the high end of that range, with actual COGS of ₪5,000–6,100, the gross margin on a ₪9,000 engagement drops to **32%** — on a service that commands 65–75% margins when scoped correctly. The operator is absorbing scope creep silently, with no mechanism to recover the cost.

The "Additional Device" tier has the same problem. A ₪3,000 add-on that takes 8 hours of labor produces a **30% gross margin** — essentially break-even once overhead is considered.

### The Market Is Paying Far More Elsewhere

Global benchmarks for in-person, expert-led crisis cybersecurity:
- **US/Western Europe:** $500–$1,000/hr for senior practitioners. An 18–32 hour engagement bills at $9,000–$32,000 USD (₪34,000–₪120,000).
- **BlackCloak** (closest SaaS analog): $3,000/year *per user* for a software subscription — no in-person, no crisis response.
- **Digital forensics** (adjacent to divorce/legal): $5,000–$15,000 per engagement. Expert witness fees: $400–$500/hr.
- **Israeli professional services adjustment:** Approximately 35–45% of US rates for senior advisory work.

**Implied Israeli fair-market range for this service: ₪15,000–₪50,000 per engagement.**

The current ₪9,000 entry price is approximately **25% of fair market value.** A price correction is not aggressive — it is alignment with what buyers in this market already pay for comparable professional expertise (attorneys, forensic accountants, private investigators).

### The "By Scope" Dead End

The Home/Office Sweep row in every pricing table reads: *"₪ by scope — 6–24h. Ask about combined packages."* This is a conversion-stopper, not a price line. Buyers in crisis do not ask follow-up questions when they are uncertain about cost — they abandon or anchor on the lowest number on the page. A published "from" price is required.

### Rapid Response Is Misframed

The current ₪4,000 Rapid Response is framed as a standalone tier, equal weight to Core Shield. But it is structurally different — it is a *time-based surcharge* for priority deployment, not a complete service package. As a tier it creates confusion: does ₪4,000 get the buyer everything, or just the fast arrival? The framing should be: a priority surcharge added to any engagement, plus a defined standalone triage option.

## Section 3 — Proposed Tier Architecture

### Design Principles

1. **Scope guarantees, not time ranges.** Every tier defines what is delivered, not how many hours it takes. The operator absorbs efficient work as profit; scope expansion is billable.
2. **Urgency is a surcharge, not a tier.** Priority scheduling is priced separately and added to any engagement.
3. **Associates unlock a family tier.** The two-operator capability enables concurrent multi-device engagements — the natural format for inheritance and multi-device divorce situations.
4. **The retainer is the highest-margin product.** It should be offered to every client at engagement close.
5. **Vertical differentiation in naming only.** The tier structure is universal; the feature descriptions and names can be vertical-specific on each page.

---

### Tier 1 — Personal Shield
*The core engagement. One person, one primary device.*

**What is included:**
- Primary device hardening (iOS, Android, or Windows/macOS laptop)
- Account lockdown: email, cloud storage, banking, social, messaging
- FIDO2 hardware key provisioning and setup
- Secure communications protocol (personal + legal team)
- Personal coaching: threat model, post-engagement habits
- Written summary for attorney file (if required)

**What is not included (explicit scope boundary):**
- Secondary devices (add-on priced separately)
- Home/office network and router
- Other family members' devices

**Time on-site:** Up to 12 hours. Work requiring more time is scoped and quoted in advance.

**Indicative price: ₪12,000**
*(Previously ₪9,000. Rationale: scope cap creates predictable delivery. Gross margin normalized to 70% vs 32–54% variable.)*

**Vertical naming variants:**
- Separation/Divorce: *Personal Shield — Separation Edition*
- Inheritance: *Personal Shield — Estate Edition*
- Business Dispute: *Personal Shield — Negotiation Edition*
- Commercial Spying: *Personal Shield — Executive Edition*

---

### Tier 2 — Personal Shield+
*One person, two devices (the most common real-world need).*

**What is included:**
- Everything in Personal Shield, applied to two devices (e.g., primary phone + laptop, or phone + tablet)
- Cross-device synchronization of secure protocols
- Device interdependency audit (how the two devices share access and data)

**Time on-site:** Up to 18 hours across one or two visits.

**Indicative price: ₪17,000**
*(Not 2 × ₪12,000. The second device benefits from work already done on the first. The buyer pays for scope, not for hours.)*

---

### Tier 3 — Family Shield *(requires associate — 2 operators)*
*Two to four family members, concurrent deployment.*

**Designed for:** Inheritance disputes with multiple beneficiaries or executors. Divorce situations involving shared custody devices or a child's device. Any situation where 2–4 people need hardening within a single legal or family event.

**What is included:**
- Two operators deployed simultaneously (Dr. Tabansky + trusted associate)
- 2–4 devices hardened in parallel
- Shared access governance: who has access to what, segregated and documented
- Family-level communications protocol (secure channel for estate/legal correspondence)
- Unified written summary for attorney/executor file

**Time on-site:** One full day (6–10 hours per operator).

**Indicative price: ₪29,000** (2 devices) — **₪36,000** (3–4 devices)
*(Associate cost included. Gross margin: 64–66%.)*

---

### Add-On: Additional Device
*Expand any Personal Shield engagement to a secondary device.*

**Scope:** 1 additional device, same session or scheduled follow-up. Up to 3 hours on-device work. FIDO2 provisioning included if not already issued.

**Indicative price: ₪4,500 per device**
*(Previously ₪3,000 with no time cap. Raised to protect margin on complex secondary devices. Gross margin: 69%.)*

---

### Add-On: Priority Scheduling Surcharge
*Urgency priced as a surcharge, not a separate service tier.*

This replaces the current "Rapid Response" tier framing. The core service is unchanged — the surcharge covers priority reservation of Dr. Tabansky's calendar and guaranteed arrival within the stated window.

| Window | Surcharge |
|---|---|
| Same day (within 4–6 hours) | +₪5,500 |
| Next day | +₪3,000 |
| 48-hour window | +₪1,500 |
| Standard scheduling (3–7 days) | No surcharge |

**Example:** A divorce client who needs hardening done before a filing date tomorrow: Personal Shield ₪12,000 + Next Day surcharge ₪3,000 = **₪15,000 total**.

**Standalone Triage Option:** For clients who are unsure of scope (e.g., "I think I've been compromised but I don't know what"), a 2–3 hour on-site assessment and written breach risk report is available at ₪5,500 standalone. The full amount is credited toward any subsequent engagement booked within 30 days.

### Home & Office Sweep
*Currently "₪ by scope" — this must be published with a floor.*

The current formulation defers conversion. Buyers in crisis do not make follow-up inquiries about price. They need a number to decide whether to proceed.

**Published pricing:**
| Scope | From (₪) |
|---|---|
| Single apartment (router, IoT, 1–2 smart screens) | ₪8,500 |
| Family home (router, cameras, smart home, 3+ rooms) | ₪14,000 |
| Small office (router, shared workstations, access points) | ₪18,000 |
| Combined: Personal Shield + apartment sweep | ₪19,000 |
| Combined: Family Shield + home sweep | ₪40,000 |

*"From" pricing with a note: final quote provided after brief phone assessment. Buyers see a floor, not a blank.*

---

### Continuity Retainer *(post-engagement only)*
*The highest-margin product in the portfolio. Available to past clients only.*

**Why this exists:** Legal proceedings last 6–24 months. Devices and accounts acquired during a separation or dispute change over time. A quarterly check-in maintains the protocols installed during the initial engagement and catches new exposures before they become vulnerabilities.

**What is included:**
- 4 × 1.5-hour quarterly check-in sessions (in-person or on-site)
- Priority WhatsApp/Signal access for urgent questions between sessions
- Annual account and access audit
- Protocol updates as operating systems or legal situation evolves

**Annual price: ₪8,000/year** (billed annually, post-engagement close)

*Gross margin: 88%. This is the product with the highest strategic value: it converts a one-time crisis client into a recurring relationship and a steady referral source.*

**Offer timing:** Presented at the close of every engagement, before the operator leaves the client's location. Not on the website — this is an in-person close.

## Section 4 — Vertical Differentiation Map

The tier structure is universal. The *emphasis* shifts by vertical.

| Vertical | Natural Entry | Most Likely Upsell | Key Differentiator to Name |
|---|---|---|---|
| Separation/Divorce | Personal Shield (₪12,000) | Add-on device (child's phone, ₪4,500) | Evidence preservation, backup lockdown |
| Inheritance Clash | Personal Shield (₪12,000) or Family Shield (₪29,000+) | Home sweep (executor's residence) | Executor access governance, attorney file protection |
| Business Dispute | Personal Shield+ (₪17,000) | Priority surcharge + add-on devices | Profile separation, M&A communication lockdown |
| Commercial Spying | Personal Shield+ + Priority (₪17,000 + ₪3,000–5,500) | Home office sweep | Rapid response, IP protection, zero-leak protocols |

**Individual-first positioning note:** Divorce and Inheritance clients are more likely to arrive via attorney referral and have more emotional purchase triggers. Business and Commercial clients are more likely to arrive via peer network and have more rational/ROI triggers. The pricing architecture supports both — the same tiers, presented with different emphasis in the copy.

## Section 5 — Price Communication Strategy

### What to Change on the Site

**1. Remove time ranges from tier headers.** "8–21 hours" communicates uncertainty, not value. Replace with scope guarantees:
- Instead of *"8–21 hours"* → *"Primary device + accounts + FIDO2 key + comms protocol"*
- Duration becomes a secondary detail under the tier, not the headline

**2. Add a value anchor above the pricing table.** One line before the table header:

> *"A single compromised negotiation in a ₪5M settlement costs more than your entire engagement. This service costs less than two hours of litigation."*

Adapt by vertical:
- Divorce: *"Your attorney is billing ₪X/hr. This engagement costs less than [N] hours of litigation."*
- Inheritance: *"Estate disputes resolved on leaked information cost families far more than prevention."*
- Commercial Spying: *"One leaked M&A term sheet costs more than this entire service."*

**3. Replace "₪ by scope" with "From ₪8,500."** Add a tooltip or footnote: *"Exact quote in 10 minutes by phone."* This gives buyers a floor without committing to a ceiling prematurely.

**4. Reframe Rapid Response as a surcharge, not a tier.** Remove it from the comparison table columns. Replace with a row at the bottom: *"Need this done urgently? Priority scheduling available — add ₪1,500–₪5,500 depending on window."*

**5. The Continuity Retainer does NOT appear on the site.** It is an in-person close, not a browsed product. Adding it to the site dilutes its intimacy and invites price comparison with alternatives.

## Section 6 — Referral Partner Economics

### The Channel Reality

Attorney and accountant referrals are the primary acquisition channel. The attorney has already done the trust-building work — their client arrives pre-qualified and pre-disposed to buy. The operator owes this channel a formal structure, not just gratitude.

### Proposed Referral Program

**Standard referral fee: ₪1,500 per converted client**, paid within 7 days of engagement completion.

At a ₪12,000 entry price, this is 12.5% — standard in Israeli professional service referral arrangements. It is small enough not to compromise the client relationship (no attorney discloses the fee to the client), but large enough to be a real income item for a referrer sending 3–6 clients per year (₪4,500–₪9,000 annually from referrals alone).

**Volume tier:** Referrers sending 4+ clients in a calendar year: ₪2,000 per referral for the whole year (retroactive from client 1).

**Alternative for referrers who decline fees (common among attorneys due to bar association rules):** Priority scheduling — clients referred by this attorney/firm receive guaranteed 48-hour booking availability regardless of calendar load. This is high perceived value at zero cash cost.

### Referral Partner Materials

Each active referral partner receives:
- A single printed card (not branded with the service's name) reading: *"If your situation involves digital security — call this number before you take any further action."* Phone number only. No URL, no service name.
- A one-page briefing sheet (for the attorney, not the client) explaining: what the service covers, what the attorney should tell their client to do before the session, and what documentation can be provided for the attorney file.

**Why no URL on the card:** Clients referred by attorneys are in crisis. A URL implies self-service browsing. A phone number implies immediate human contact. The WhatsApp/Signal first-contact model is the right entry for this persona — the card reinforces it.

### Accountant Referrals (secondary channel)

Business dispute and inheritance clients often arrive via accountants and financial advisors rather than attorneys. The same fee structure applies. Accountants are typically more comfortable accepting referral fees than attorneys (bar association constraints apply only to licensed attorneys).
